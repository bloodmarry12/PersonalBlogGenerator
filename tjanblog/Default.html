Greetings, guys and gals. Welcome again to my blog site, a place to demo all my ideas on Internet. From certain view, this blog works like a lab or a garage to me. I believe that all developers will agree on the necessity of an online access to their documents, codes, services and stuff like that. Well, at long last, I have my own access now.

Something about me.

I, myself, am a sort of simple guy. Nothing in my life was so special that need to be mentioned about, which actually pains me quite a lot, you know. As a programmer, it is a shame that I cannot introduce myself in form like "the author of SOMETHING". Of course, I did wrote some pieces during my college time, working days and spare time. The only problem is that the author, that is me, is not satisfied with these works. This, as part of the reason, leads to this blog -- I believe that it is the time to make a difference.

As a programmer, I classify myself into so called ployglot type. Like many people in China, Pascal and C lead me to the world of programming at my middle school time. C++ came naturally to me in my college days. Right on the 2015 New Year Day, I quitted my position as a C++ developer in a power grid automation vendor. From then on, I started my F# days.

Technically speaking, I am still a will-be ployglot. Writing simple codes in certain language does not make you a this-language programmer. Otherwise, I believe there will be a huge number of HTML programmers, CSS programmers, or Javascript programmers. An insight and understanding of the logic using certain programming language is the very baseline of mastering a programming language. With this standard, I am just a C++/F# bilingual (a poor one maybe).

I am on my way of mastering Racket, Haskell and Rust languages while diving deeper into C++ and F#. Recently, cloud computing and machine learning have come to my view.

What will there be here?

Mainly blog posts of course. But something I have to say is that as a Chinese programmer with just common English training in my middle school and university, most of my posts will be written in 中文 rather than English. You can check the contents of my blog posts using the topbar Contents button.

And I plan to share some spare time project entries in this blog. Besides the description blog post, visitors may find the entries by clicking the Tests button on the topbar of this page.

This site is created with a self-written static site generator. This is an exercising F# project from the book F# Deep Dives at first. But it is part of my automatic building and deploying system now. Therefore, just like other live projects, you may expect more features, different appearance or even new site structure here.

So, you want a talk?

Oh, if you want to contact with me. You can find my email and Weibo (a popular Twitter like tool in China) number in the Contact page. Contact will be updated in time. I am looking forward to the conversation with you.
